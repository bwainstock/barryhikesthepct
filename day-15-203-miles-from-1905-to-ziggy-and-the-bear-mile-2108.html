Day 15 - 20.3 miles from 190.5 to Ziggy and the Bear (Mile 210.8)

This morning was our coldest morning yet. We expected that as we camped around 8000 feet, but it was still impressive just how cold it was.

After warming up in the few rays of sun peering through the trees, we descended down Snow Creek Canyon on our way to the desert floor at 1100 feet. When we reached 6300 feet the pine trees and squirrels turned back into desert chaparral and lizards. The trail was washed out in parts and was covered in fist sized rocks. Hiking was mentally intensive and we really had to pay attention to our footsteps.

Even though the trail was basically a series of switchbacks, one after another for twenty miles, the views were awesome. Out in front of us was the Coachella Valley with views of Morongo Casino and the Mesa Wind Farm. Behind us loomed the snow speckled San Jacinto Peak.

Around 2 o'clock we reached the bottom of the mountain and refilled our water. The last five miles took us across the valley and under Interstate 80. The wind whipping through the valley made this portion of the hike incredibly frustrating and the coarse sand didn't help ease the pain.

By five o'clock we reached the trail angel house that Ziggy and the Bear run where they offer showers, basic resupply, and covered sleeping areas. After the day's hiking, the Little Caesars pizza they picked up tasted amazing.

Like all of the other hiker hangouts, we enjoyed seeing everyone wander in throughout the evening and traded hiker stories. Tomorrow we'll start our climb through the San Bernardino Mountains on our way to Big Bear.
