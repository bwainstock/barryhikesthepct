Day 35 - 21.7 miles from Highway 58 to mile 588.2

This morning the breakfast room was filled with even more hikers than yesterday. Purple Princess, Dunzo, Honey Stick, Starfish, Junior, and plenty more were around. Unfortunately, when we tried Big Al for a ride, he didn't answer. There were plenty more trail angels in town, however, and we were able to get to the trailhead by 9AM. It was already hot outside and we found Liability, Sticky Buns, and Dreaded Mustache setting off right in front of us.

After about a mile of flat trail, we climbed 2000 feet into the Tehachapi Mountains through coarse sand. The hot, sandy hills coupled with a fresh, heavy resupply and 6 liters of water meant slow going this morning. Once we made it into the mountains, the temperature cooled off and we had a bit more tree cover. We walked along a tree lined dirt road for most of the morning until we stopped under a large oak tree for lunch. Kale, Liability, Dreaded Milkshake, and Sticky Buns joined us while I napped through the early afternoon heat.
Around 2:30PM, we left our lunch spot and hiked through the rocky, high desert hills. We reached our water supply for the day around 5 o'clock and found a large group of hikers huddled around. The source was basically a cow trough so the quality wasn't the highest. While sitting around filtering, a few cows stopped by to take their fill.
About five more miles of hiking through another burn zone saw us to our final destination: a makeshift campsite in a small ravine. We plan to hike close to thirty miles tomorrow with the hope of getting into Kennedy Meadows sooner than later.
