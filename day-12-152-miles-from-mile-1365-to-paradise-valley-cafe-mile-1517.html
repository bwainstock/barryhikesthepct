Day 12 - 15.2 miles from mile 136.5 to Paradise Valley Cafe (Mile 151.7)

The trend of early mornings continues. This is probably the norm now. By the time we got out of camp around 7 it must have already been 80 degrees. It was hot! Our water source was a half mile from camp and then we started our ascent.

By 11 o'clock we were getting hungry and started to look for a shady spot to rest. In the distance we saw a large tent setup and we decided to investigate. It was more trail magic! Mary from Anza was getting ready to grill hamburgers, hotdogs, and marinated pork loin. She also had ice cold water, juice, and soda. This was hiker heaven. Our short break once again turned into a multi hour stop as 12%, the Beaverton Brothers, and a lot more hikers streamed in.
The last eight miles flew by as we climbed our way to Highway 78 where we planned to hitchhike to the Paradise Valley Cafe. Mike from Anza quickly picked us up. Mike was a kindergarten teacher and had a very small apple orchard at his home. After hearing that we were PCT hikers , he offered us a stay at his house complete with home-cooked meal, showers, and laundry. Unfortunately, Matt didn't feel comfortable taking the offer and we just had Mike take us to the restaurant.
We both got giant burgers and ate dinner with Purple Princess and Hoat. Paradise Valley Cafe lets hikers camp on their property so we stayed the night and plan to hitch into Idyllwild after breakfast tomorrow.
