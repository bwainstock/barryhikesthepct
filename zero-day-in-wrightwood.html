Zero Day in Wrightwood

As expected, we all woke up before the rest of town and quickly ran to the local cafe. We walked through the door just as the opened and were quickly joined by other hikers. Our day consisted of resupplying our food bags, multiple visits to the local hardware store which acted as the hub of hiker activity, eating more food, visiting the post office, and eating a third meal somewhere between lunch and dinner.

In the late afternoon we called our new friend Steve to arrange dinner and he picked us up from our motel about an hour later. Steve took us back to his house and introduced us to his wonderful family: his wife Shannon and his four children, Connor, Cameron, Madison, and JB. After spending time in their lovely home and getting to know each other, we drove around the block to the family's friend's house. We were introduced to Carlin and his family. Carlin was a master smoker and he showed us the pork butt and ribs he was preparing. Even Frankenstein's mouth was watering, even though he'd been a vegetarian for many years.

Along with the amazing smoked meats, Carlin had whipped up homemade BBQ sauce, coleslaw, mac 'n cheese, and apple pie. To top it off, he was very generous with his whiskey cupboard and we all felt we'd died and gone to heaven. After almost four weeks on trail, it was really great to sit around a warm home with great people and fantastic food. Our stomachs and hearts are full of love and we're all smiling uncontrollably.

After dinner and dessert, Steve and Shannon took us back to their home and showed us to our rooms. The kids all piled into their guest room and their rooms were lent to each of us hikers. Steve offered to cook us breakfast tomorrow and drive us to the trail whenever we need it. He plans to be awake fairly early so we'll hopefully get to the trail by about 7 o'clock.
