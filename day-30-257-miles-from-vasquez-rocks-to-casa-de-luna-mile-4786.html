Day 30 - 25.7 miles from Vasquez Rocks to Casa De Luna (Mile 478.6)

Out of Vasquez Rocks picnic area and through the town of Agua Dulce a little over an hour. The trail actually turns into the road through town for a few miles which made hiking fairly fast. From town we climbed 2000 feet back into the hills of the Angeles National Forest. We were surrounded by green hillsides in every direction.

Yesterday's short hike and early night left me feeling energized. A minor milestone for thru-hikers is hitting 10 miles by 10AM and we got to our first water source at the 10.5 mile mark just before 10AM. After a break for water and second/third breakfast, we continued our walk through the lush green hills. The terrain wasn't particularly exciting, but it was a nice day with easy hiking.

The quick morning meant we got to our final destination around 5 o'clock. We had passed a man out for a run within the last couple of miles of our hike and he came back to the trailhead as we were trying to hitch into Casa De Luna. He waved us over to his car and told us he knew exactly where we were going.

We pulled up to Casa De Luna shortly after getting picked up and the driver walked right over to Joe and Terrie Anderson, the trail angels that lived here. Apparently everyone in this small town knows each other. The Anderson's provide camping in their manzanita forest backyard, showers, charging stations, beer, and best of all, dinner and breakfast. We quickly found a spot in the forest to setup camp and headed back out to hang with the rest of the hikers.

We met a southbound hiker who was finishing the trail in sections named GoalTech and a large group of hikers we already knew were sitting around the front yard chatting. Starfish, Wendy Bird, Junior, Honey Stick, Velcro, FM, Andy, Vince, and all sorts of other hikers were drinking and laughing and soaking in the good vibes. This is a great stop along the trail and a fantastic place to forget about some of the aches and pains of the trail.

It'll be hard to leave, but tomorrow we've got to get out of The Anderson's early. Breakfast is at 6:30 and we plan to leave shortly after. There is another fire closure on the trail ahead and we plan to hitchhike around it. If we can get a hitch in time, we hope to walk about 24 miles to our final destination, Hiker Town. If we can't get a hitch in time, we'll just hitchhike all the way to Hiker Town and miss the 500 mile marker.
