Day 37 - 21.3 miles from mile 617.7 to mile 638

After more than 600 miles of fairly gentle desert, we were finally hitting unrelenting dry terrain. We planned for another early morning because of the heat and got started on our hike up and over the first set of hills.

Kale had warned us the other day that this part of the trail would be frustrating. The climb wasn't all that bad, climbing less than 1000 feet over two miles and then flattening out for the next four. The issue came from the never ending sand. It felt like trying to hike uphill on the beach. It felt very much like one step forward, two steps back. Together with the already sweltering heat, this morning sapped our energy.

By midday we had made it to mile 631 at Bird Spring Pass. We had heard there was a water cache here and we came to find multiple jugs of water and lounge chairs. There were plenty of hikers waiting out the heat here and we gladly joined them. While Bird and I napped in the shade, Matt pushed on and climbed the 1500 feet out of the pass.

After our two hour nap, Bird and I followed Matt up the mountain. It turns out two hours wasn't quite long enough and the sun was still blistering hot. The climb was arduous and it felt like it took forever.

Halfway up the climb we met a man out for a day hike going the opposite way as us. He told us we were the 52nd and 53rd people he'd seen that day. I had never really thought about what it was like outside of our bubble of hikers. Five miles ahead of us was a completely different group of people hiking at the same speed as is and we'd never see them.

Shortly after the peak of the climb, we found Matt taking his turn in the shade and we took a sidetrail to Yellow Jacket Spring. This spring was referred to as a seep and was more of a refilling puddle than a flowing stream. We dipped our bottles in the puddle and waited in the shade a bit longer.

This was the first time we had cell phone service in a long time. Matt took the opportunity to call his mother to coordinate our pickup from Kennedy Meadows. We are all getting off trail to go to a friend's wedding. Along with picking us up in Kennedy Meadows, Jan is going to drop us off a food resupply tomorrow in Walker Pass. This will save us from having to hitch 45 minutes each way into Onyx.

After the phone call, we continued about one mile farther and setup camp in the middle of some oak trees. We were once again exhausted and quickly setup camp. Tomorrow will see us hiking into Walker Pass and right back out on our way to Kennedy Meadows. We'd like to be there around lunch so we'll have another early morning.
