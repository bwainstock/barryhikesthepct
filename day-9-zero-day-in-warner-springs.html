Day 9 - Zero Day In Warner Springs

Pia, Me, and Big Spoon cook up burgers for the masses.

Slowly but surely the campground woke up starting at 6 o'clock. Some of the hikers were getting an early start while others were waiting for the Resource Center to open for breakfast. At 8 o'clock, the volunteers arrived and announced that they couldn't serve food because they were having permit issues. Apparently a community member called in a complaint.

This was pretty deflating and most of the hikers not zeroing left soon after. Those of us that were zeroing just hung around and relaxed. We enjoyed the air-conditioned Resource Center with Honey Stick, Anna, and Pia as we all soaked our feet and traded hiking stories.

City inspectors were in and out all day and it seemed like things weren't looking good. Their kitchen had been okayed by the county, but their permit was not current.

Slowly through the day the previous day's hikers were replaced with new ones. It was sad to see the people we'd been hiking with for the past week leave, but it was fun to meet a whole new group.

Since there was no hot food being served, we asked if we could buy the frozen hamburgers and fixings and grill it on the charcoal bbq outside the Center. For whatever reason, they are okay with this and we jumped into action.

Sinatra and friends hitched to the store and bought charcoal. Pia jumped on the grill and I worked on gathering all of the food. Big Spoon quickly joined to help and we started a hamburger list. Just about the whole campground signed up and we cooked up 36 burgers.

After cleanup, I was exhausted and laid low with Pretzel and Malibu. 8 o'clock rolled around, aka Hiker Midnight, and everyone naturally went to sleep.

Tomorrow we'll take a nearo and hike about ten miles in the afternoon. My knee is starting to feel better and the swelling is going down in Matt's leg.
