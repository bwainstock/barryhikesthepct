Day 7 - 17.5 miles from Scissor's Crossing to a patch of dirt at mile 94.5

Today was supposed to be a hot day and uncovered for most of the way so we decided to get out of camp as early as possible. By 7 o'clock we were packed up and out of camp. We walked down the road back the trailhead with Starfish and eventually got a hitch.

When we got to the trail, a trail angel was handing out fresh fruit and Cactus Cooler which made the already scorching heat a bit more bearable.

Our hike started with a climb over the first few miles and then flattened out for the rest of the time. Yucca and cactus dominated the landscape the whole time. Once the trail flattened out, the day was a trudge. We followed the contours of the mountain for about 15 miles. In and out, in and out, but always in direct sunlight.
At mile 91 we hit the Third Gate water cache. Because there was no running water, someone had brought about 50 gallons of water out to the middle of nowhere. This became a natural stopping point for everyone and there were hikers taking a break all over.

After a two hour siesta to wait out the midday sun, we headed out to finish the last three miles of our day. We finished our hike at mile 94.5 and setup camp by 6 o'clock. A hiker named Mad Science was already in camp and we watched as Honey Stick, Diogenes, and a few others trickled in. Tomorrow we want to get started even earlier so we're hoping to get to bed early tonight.

My knee is still really bothering me and Matt's shin is severely swollen. We think I've got an IT band problem and he's got bad shin splints. Tomorrow will be a slow hobble into the Warner Springs Resource Center where we'll be able to get a hot meal and camp for free.
