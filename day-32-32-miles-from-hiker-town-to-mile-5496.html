Day 32 - 32 miles from Hiker Town to mile 549.6

After a decent night's sleep in a real bed in Hiker Town, we were on the trail by 5:45AM with 32 miles ahead of us. The first 18 or so miles along the Aqueduct were flat and easy. We were expected boring as well, but the sunrise and views across the Mojave Desert provided for a great hike.

While Bird and I stopped periodically to snack or catch our breath, Matt powered through the morning all the way to the first water stop 17 miles in. Bird and I stopped here around 11:45 for lunch just as Matt was leaving. He'd already been there for close to an hour. This was the last we saw of him until camp later at night.
The water was at the start of the PCT famous wind farm and we could already tell the end of the day was going to be more difficult than the beginning. Gusting winds slowed our hike down considerably and the clouds hanging over the desert mountains misted on us periodically.

Along with the wind, we were climbing into the desert hills away from the flat desert floor. Our first stop in the hills was Tylerhorse Canyon where we intended to refill our water again. Where we expected a strong flowing creek, we found a trickle of water which we had to scoop using our pots to get it into our water bottles. After a short break here with Taxi and Hornbuckle, we continued on through the hills.

By now, we were both pretty tired and the climbs were getting harder. The wind was still gusting and the trail was sandy, which made every step harder than the last. We passed a big campsite about a quarter mile before our intended stopping point and said "Hi" to our friends Doogan and Sweetums. Thankfully, the last quarter mile was downhill and we finally reached camp, 32 miles farther than we were this morning.

We were hoping that this side of the hill would be out of the wind, but no luck tonight. We're camped between bushes which should help a bit, but tonight will be a tough night to sleep through. Tomorrow leaves us with 8 miles into Tehachapi where we'll have a motel room to sleep and shower and do laundry. We'll take a zero day in Tehachapi as well, so we can catch up on any rest we don't get tonight.
