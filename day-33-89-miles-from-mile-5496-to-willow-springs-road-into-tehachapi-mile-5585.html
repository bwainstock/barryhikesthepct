Day 33 - 8.9 miles from mile 549.6 to Willow Springs Road into Tehachapi (Mile 558.5)

We planned yesterday's long hike so that today would be quick and painless. The 9 miles we hiked today were mostly downhill and we got an early enough start that the weather wasn't too warm. Other than still hiking through a massive wind farm, our plan worked out and we made it to Willow Springs Road by 10AM.

Once we got to the road, we found a long list of trail angels posted and quickly gave the number next to Big Al's name a call. While waiting for Big Al to come pick us up, we booked a room at the Best Western and chatted with Hornbuckle and Wind Screen as they waited for a hitch.

Big Al picked us up within 15 minutes and drove us into Tehachapi. It seemed like his whole day was planned around giving hikers rides. Big Al offered to take us to the post office and pick us up when we were done to drop us off at our hotel.

After finishing up our business at the post office, we headed to the hotel and got to work on our standard town chores. Showers, naps, and food were all standards. We walked off our town lunch and stopped into the Tehachapi Museum to see what the local history was all about. Mary, the museum steward, showed us pictures from her backyard of California Condor chicks and gigantic elk.

Tehachapi also has a movie theater so we decided we would spend the evening watching the new Mad Max. About two minutes in my body decided I needed more sleep and I missed most the movie. We finished the night off with delicious BBQ and got to bed early.

We're staying in town another full day tomorrow. The next 8 or so miles of the trail continue through the wind farm and don't have any water sources. We're planning to call Big Al again for a ride to the trail and slackpacking this section. Slackpacking means leaving most of your pack in town or with a trail angel and only taking minimal weight. We should be able to fly through these 8 miles and get back to town for more rest. We still need to resupply our food and do laundry.
