12.4 miles from mile 357 to Highway 2 into Wrightwood (Mile 369.4)

This morning, we woke up to cold, but sunny blue skies. The impending storm hadn't hit us yet and we were able to break camp still dry. Town adrenaline was in our blood and we were ready to push it. The cold helped us break camp early and quickly and we were on trail by 6:30.

Snow started to fall about an hour into the hike as we climbed up the ridge line. We climbed about 1000' out of camp to the top of the mountain where the wind had really picked up. As we climbed, the air grew colder, the wind blew harder, and we had trouble catching our breath as the altitude started to affect us. Ice crystals were painfully whipping our faces. Bird, Matt, and myself were all pretty miserable and had to take short breaks every few minutes. We weren't alone in our misery though, as Junior, Happy Feet, and Colin all stopped with us along the way.

About six miles into the hike we passed by Mountain High Ski Resort, the resort I used to snowboard at when I was younger. This unexpected landmark perked up my spirits and helped me along for the last few miles. By noon, we reached the road where we were to hitch hike into town. Hikers streamed out of forest onto road and waited for hitch. About thirty minutes without seeing a car made us nervous that the road had been closed due to snow.

At first, we only saw CalTrans snow plows and then a convoy of forest service volunteers. The three cars all stopped to pick up the fourteen hikers waiting in the cold. We would later learn that the three cars ended up driving back and forth to the trailhead for the rest of the day helping out the hikers.
Our driver was gracious enough to drop us off at our motel where we met up with Frankenstein, showered, did laundry, stuffed our stomachs with delicious Mexican food, and generally lazed around. Later at night we went to the local bar and played pub trivia. We were the only hikers in the bar and ended up winning by one strategic point. The second place team chatted with us about hiking and invited us to their home for dinner and a place to stay tomorrow. They were extremely nice people and we are all excited for a home cooked meal and a comfortable house.

Tomorrow we need to run town errands, resupply our food bags, pick up my new shoes, and of course eat food. The snow is still falling and hikers who left town yesterday have turned back. Hikers who were stuck a day or two behind us have hitched into town and out of the bad weather. This means town is packed and we're starting to run into friends who aren't quite on our hiking schedule.
