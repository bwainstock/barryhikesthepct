Day 16 - 21.4 miles from 210.8 to 232.2

The wind last night at the trail angel house was outrageous. The tarps that were setup to cover the hikers shook all night and I probably got my worst night of sleep yet. We finally got up with all is the other hikers around 5:30 and ate our left over pizza. By 7 o'clock we had packed and said goodbye and started our 1500 ft climb out of the Coachella Valley.

The temperature was mild, but the wind was still screaming. We walked along a grassy ridge for a bit and finally descended into Whitewater Preserve. This area used to be trout farm and is now a nature preserve with a fantastic ranger station offering camping and water. We wished we had camped here instead of Ziggy and the Bear.
After taking a break here and refilling our water, we climbed about 3000 ft and followed more exposed grassy ridges. The heat had finally picked up and the day dragged.
Just when we were about done with the ridge and the heat, we dropped down to Mission Creek and followed along until camp. We found Pia, who now goes by Bird, Frankenstein, and Lucas already at the campsite and decided it was a good time to stop.
Tomorrow we start a pretty major climb into the San Bernardino Mountains on our way to Big Bear.
