Day 31 - Nero from Casa De Luna to Hiker Town (Mile 517.6)

We didn't get our hitch in time. That tells most of the story of the day. After a nice pancake breakfast at The Anderson's, Terri drove us back to the road where we were to hitch. Since most of the cars on the rode were morning commuters, getting a ride was pretty tough and we walked along for about 5 miles. Eventually, we were picked up by a nice guy in a pickup truck and he took us as far as The Historic Rock Inn in Lake Hughes. We stopped in around 10AM to eat a second breakfast and to figure out what our options were with fellow hiker Hornbuckle.

  • Option 1
    • Walk 12 miles from The Rock Inn to Hiker Town along the road
  • Option 2
    • Walk four miles along the road to the end of the fire closure and walk 22 miles of trail to Hiker Town
  • Option 3
    • Hitch the four miles to the end of the fire closure and walk 22 miles of trail to Hiker Town
  • Option 4
    • Hitch all the way into Hiker Town

Both Bird and I really dislike road walking as it's typically boring, dangerous, and hard on the knees. Hornbuckle and Matt didn't mind the road walk and didn't particularly want to walk the full 20 plus miles to Hiker Town. Bird and I decided we'd try to walk to trail if we could get a hitch in time, but that we'd hitch all the way to Hiker Town if we couldn't. Hornbuckle and Matt decided to just road walk into Hiker Town.

As Bird and I left the restaurant, we walked along the road with our thumbs out with our friend Liability trailing behind us about 100 yards. We didn't have much luck for the first hour or so and continued walking. Our trail walking deadline passed and we resigned to just hitching directly into Hiker Town. This would give us another easy day to sort out further logistics. Bird needed to buy new shoes and I planned to update my blog since I'd been slacking.

A nice man named Steve, who worked at the Christian camp in town, picked us up on his way to an appointment. He told us he would have to stop at his appointment for about an hour, but if we stayed on the road, he'd pick us back up and take us where we needed to go. Liability gave us his backpack to take with us which made his road walk much easier. When Steve dropped us off to go to his appointment, we left our bags in his car and kept walking down the road. This made for a rather enjoyable, leisurely walk along the highway.

By about 1 o'clock, Steve had picked us back up and we reached Hiker Town. This property was the work of an eccentric man who built a replica Western town out of old movie set pieces. The garage had been converted into a hiker lounge with laundry, showers, and couches to sit around on. Small shacks and shipping containers surrounded the property for hikers to sleep in and there were spots to camp if we wanted to. We decided to rent out one of the small rooms and had a generally lazy afternoon.

Mad Science was in the garage lounge when we got there and it was nice to catch up with him. Later in the day, most of the hikers we'd seen at the Anderson's trickled into Hiker Town and we all ate and drank together.

Tomorrow we hike 18 flat miles along the California Aqueduct. Most of the hikers here plan to put in 30 or more miles to make the next day relatively short into Tehachapi. We've got 32 miles planned in our minds which means a 5:30AM departure time.
