Day 14 - 9.6 miles from Devil's Slide Trail to campsite at 190.5

Today we needed to finish up a few errands and make our way out of town.

After eating breakfast at the delicious Red Kettle with Pia and 12%, we picked up a few packages that we had waiting for us at the post office. Matt's girlfriend, Cassie, sent us homemade cookies and a few dehydrated dinners which was awesome.

To supplement the cookies we picked up a few other things at the local market and checked out of our hotel. After saying goodbye to all of the hikers sticking around for a bit longer, we hitched to the bottom of Devil's Slide Trail and started hiking.

Devil's Slide Trail isn't part of the PCT, but it's the way of out Idyllwild. It's also the steepest trail we've climbed so far, gaining a bit under 2000 feet in about 2.5 miles. The trail connects to the PCT at around 9000 feet in beautiful pine forest. Our plan was to follow the trail to San Jacinto Peak and climb up to 10800 ft, but the sky was looking quite dark and a few rain drops had begun to fall.

We decided to skip the peak and continued to mile 186 to fill our water at a creek fed by snow melt. We'd already seen a few left over snow patches and now we were drinking ice cold snow melt.
Off to the north of the mountain we could see Cabazon, Morongo Casino, and the Mesa Wind Farm down at 1200 feet. By 7:30 we hit camp and ended the night with Velcro, Sebaba, Spider, and a number of other hikers.
Tomorrow we'll hike 20 miles down to the desert floor and over to the trail angel house Ziggy and the Bear. We'll also pass the 200 mile mark which feels like a pretty major accomplishment.
