Day 27 - 27.3 miles from mile 394 to Mill Creek Fire Station (Mile 418.6)

The cold evening left frost on the outside of our tents and gave us a reason to break camp quickly to build up some body heat this morning. We had to take a 2.7 mile side trail out of the campground down into Cooper Canyon. The trail was chilly, but covered in soft, healthy soil and lined with vibrant green foliage. Just as we descended into the canyon, we climbed right back out and crossed Highway 2 three times.

As we stopped for a snack break at the first crossing, we noticed a trail angel on the other side of the road handing out goodies. The trail angel had driven from near LAX airport to give out less obvious supplies like Chapstick, hot sauce, first aid supplies, and Spam. After a nice 45 minute break we continued down through the San Gabriel Mountains and passed mile 400.

At mile 402 we passed a nonprofit Boy Scout Camp called Camp Glenwood. Initially, we had planned to refill our water here and move on. However, just as we were setting our bags down, one of the caretakers, Max, was firing up his grill to cook hamburgers and invited us to stay for lunch. This lead to another hour long break where we chatted about the drivers on Highway 2 and the car wreckage you can find throughout the hills from corners being taken too tightly. We thanked Max for his kindness and kept climbing down through miles of poodle dog bush. We hit more trail magic around noon: hot dogs and fruit! Apparently weekends in Angeles National Forest are a good time for city folk to help out hungry hikers.
Later in the day we climbed up 1500 feet and back down 1500 feet to Mill Creek Fire Station. The trail was windy, but well covered and never got too hot. We showed up to a windy picnic area around 7 and setup camp next to about 10 other hikers. Because of the wind, we ate dinner in front of the concrete bathrooms and chatted with an Australian hiker named Crash. Soon after dinner we climbed into our tents and quickly fell asleep.

Today was our first marathon day feel and we felt it. Tomorrow we're hoping to put in another big mile day.
