Day 41 - 8.7 miles from Manter Creek to Kennedy Meadows (Mile 702.4)

After two full days back on trail, I slept really well and woke excited. Kennedy Meadows was in our sights and we should be there by 10 o'clock. Within the first mile, we could see open meadows on horizon.

Today took us through easy rolling sandy hills to the south fork of the Kern River where we stopped for snacks. We didn't need water so kept hiking after a short break. The trail finally hit the meadows and our pace picked up. Large peaks and pine trees filled the horizon and we finally passed mile 700.
The final 2.4 miles were a blur and we walked directly to the Kennedy Meadows General Store due a hamburger and soda. About 40 other hikers were here building up the strength to start their journey into the mountains.

We'll stay the night here, pickup our food resupply, and pack our bear cans. The Sierra mountains require bear proof cans for all of your food. This adds an extra two pounds to the backpack and will take some getting used to.
