22.6 miles from mile 335.6 to 357

An extremely lazy morning saw Matt and Frankenstein pass the campsite Bird and I stopped at last night. They decided to head to McDonald's a bit earlier than us to get in a few extra calories before lunch. Bird and I left camp around 8:30 and hiked a fairly easy seven miles to McDonald's. The morning view off of the hill we were hiking down was gorgeous and the promise of air conditioning and food made us giddy.
We strode into McDonald's around 10:30 and found the restaurant packed with hikers, save for a few normal people who pulled off of highway 15 to stretch their legs and get out of morning traffic. Hashbrowns, chicken nuggets, french fries, a chocolate milkshake, and plenty of Diet Coke kept me occupied for three hours. We all checked our email, read through the news, and chatted with the rest of the hikers about the past few weeks and the upcoming town stop in Wrightwood. The weather forecast was calling for rain and possibly snow so the four of us booked a motel room before leaving McDonald's.
The first few miles out of McDonald's passed through beautiful sandstone rock formations with views of train tracks winding throughout the landscape. After this relatively flat section, we had a hard climb up towards Wrightwood. From about 3000' near Cajon pass to 7500' with a six mile walk on a dirt road to skip a section of poisonous poodle dog bush left us pretty tired. The calories from McDonald's got us most of the way up the mountain, but the last few miles left Bird and Matt pretty exhausted.
Even with a late start and a lengthy McDonald's stop, we still managed to hike 22 miles today. It was mentaly and physically challenging, but the campsite we've stopped at is worth it. The storm that is expected looks to be coming in tomorrow morning so we plan to get an early start tomorrow and get to town as soon as possible.
