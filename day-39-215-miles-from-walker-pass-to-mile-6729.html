Day 39 - 21.5 miles from Walker Pass to mile 672.9

After what felt like a long, but needed break from hiking, we're finally back on trail. Our friend Frankenstein drove from Orange County to pick us up and take us back to Walker Pass. An uneventful drive down I5 took us to the campground where we ate dinner and camped one last time with Frankenstein.

Waking up in the morning, we noticed there were about 15 other hikers camped out. This brought us right back into the hiking mindset and we quickly packed up and got ready to go. We said goodbye to Frankenstein and thanked him over and over before finally heading back out to hike.

The first eight miles took us up a 2300 foot climb into the Owens Peak Wilderness. Sandy hills patched with tufts of golden grass turned into trees as we climbed. Swarms of grasshoppers in the trees buzzed like tazers as they fluttered around us.

A few miles down the other side of the mountain we stopped at Joshua Tree spring for water. The hikers that were already there told us they had just chased away a bear also getting water. We decided to chance it and headed down to the spring. If the threat of a bear wasn't going to stop is, nor was the sign warning us about uranium.

We ate lunch in the shade and napped for two hours through the midday heat. As we woke, more hikers filtered in and we decided it was time to leave. Just as we were headed out, the bear came back for another drink. We turned around just to get a glimpse and quickly left.

The day finished as it had started with a 2000 don't climb over the last five miles. Part way up the climb we passed the quarter mark of the trail. The path was shaded, but covered in granite shards which made for a slow hike. The scenery felt like we were finally getting to the Sierra.

We will try to put in another full day tomorrow leaving just a few more miles afterwards to get into Kennedy Meadows. Then we'll really be entering the next phase of the trail.
