Day 11 - 17 miles from mile 119.5 to a campsite at mile 136.5

The plan for the day was to hike eight miles to our next water source and then nine more miles to camp. We're still taking our time so we got out of camp early and really watched our pace.

Our water for the day was supposed to come from a water tank near a trail angel's house. When we got there, it turns out the house not only had water, but food, beer, shade, and a pretty awesome trail angel host. Mike Herrera runs a desert hiker oasis and allows hikers to camp in his yard. Our 30 minute water break turned into a three hour siesta and it was amazing. Thanks Mike!

After our midday nap, we hopped back on trail and descended 2000 feet to mile 136.5 by 8:45. Tonight is the first night we are camping by ourselves which is pretty amazing.

Tomorrow we will get to mile 151 and the trail famous Paradise Valley Cafe. We'll stay the night on the restaurant's lawn and then hitchhike into Idyllwild. Most years you can hike into Idyllwild, but a recent fire along the trail has forced an alternate route along the highway. This didn't sound appealing to us so we'll hitch into town and stay the night.
