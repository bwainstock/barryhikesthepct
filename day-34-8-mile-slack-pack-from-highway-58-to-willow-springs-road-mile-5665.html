Day 34 - 8 mile slack pack from Highway 58 to Willow Springs Road (Mile 566.5)

After a big breakfast with Crash, Big Ups, and a few other hikers in the hotel's breakfast room, we called Big Al for a ride to the trail head. Geared up with only a few snacks and water, we were excited to fly through the trail today.

Big Al told us it would be easier to get picked up from Willow Springs Road at mile 558.5 instead of from our original finishing point for the day, so we decided to hike backwards for this section. Big Al dropped us off at the highway near mile 566.5 and we got to walking. The trail was very similar to the last few days with sand under our feet and desert shrubs speckling the hillsides.

With little weight on our backs, we flew up the 1000 foot climb. A fellow hiker, Kale, was doing the same thing as us today, but in reverse. We caught him just as he was finishing the hill and he told us his parents were picking him up from the highway. Kale assured us that the rest of the trail was fairly flat and we would have no problems for the rest of the hike. The hill was also the first time we'd seen horses on the trail. The whole trail is supposed to be graded for horses and we've seen evidence of horse use, but this was the first time we've seen the actual animals.

The rest of the hike was uneventful and we made it to the road within a few hours. While waiting once again for Big Al to pick us up, we chatted with a few locals out for a run on the PCT. Running Deer and his friends were very excited that we were out hiking and reminded us, no matter the circumstances of the day, to enjoy the music of the wilderness. Even though the wind could be pretty frustrating in this section, the sounds it made were quite beautiful.

After our slackpack, our town day was pretty standard. We resupplied our food bags, ate lunch, finished laundry, and watched another movie. We met up with Tater, Darko, and a couple of their friends for pizza and called it a night.

Tomorrow starts the hike into Kennedy Meadows which means the unofficial end of the desert. Unfortunately, this last stretch is looking to be pretty warm and doesn't have a lot of water, which means heavy carries. We'll eat another breakfast at the hotel tomorrow morning and call Big Al for one more ride.
