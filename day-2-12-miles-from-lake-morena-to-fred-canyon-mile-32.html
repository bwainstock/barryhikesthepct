Day 2 - 12 Miles from Lake Morena to Fred Canyon (Mile 32)

We were able to casually wake up and "lounge" around this morning since we had to wait until noon for the kickoff event tot start. We were even able to walk about a quarter mile down the road and eat breakfast at the Lake Morena Malt Shop.

After some deliciously basic eggs and toast, we found the kickoff event posted the schedule for the day. Unfortunately, it turns out today was pretty light on talks and was mostly registration and dinner. We picked up a PCT 2015 bandana, checked out some of the vendors, and decided to hit the trail. All in all, kind of disappointing.

We got out of Lake Morena at about 2 o'clock and figured we could hike about twelve miles before sundown. This would leave us with a relatively mild ten mile hike tomorrow to get to our first town, Mt. Laguna.

The first two miles skirted the lake and went through very coarse sand which was frustrating. Eventually, we dipped under a road and followed along for the next few hours.

By four o'clock we passed through a horse campground where we refilled our water. Seven more miles up around a mountain took us to our campsite at Fred Canyon where we found three other hikers getting ready for bed. We quickly setup camp and ate dinner.
My legs are sore and my right knee hurts more than I'd like. The shorter hike tomorrow is welcome and we'll resupply and camp in town.
