Day 40 - 20.8 miles from mile 672.9 to Manter Creek (Mile 693.7)

Today started with a nice change of pace. We were starting by descending instead of climbing a giant hill. The morning was overcast, but still warm. The first goal was a campground about eight miles out where we'd fill water from a spigot and eat a snack.

When we got to the campground entrance, there was a sign saying the spigot was on the far end, over a mile away! Thankfully Chimney Creek was nearby and we decided to break there instead.

We met hikers new to us Crunchberry and Stayin Alive here and ate snacks. After filling our water bottles, we left around 11 o'clock to start a 2400 foot climb. The trail was more coarse desert sand with gentle shade. About five miles in, we stopped under a large oak tree with low branches for lunch and a quick nap.
We finished our final hour of climbing and descended the other side of the hill. Sparse trees turned into a burn area and our shade disappeared. After the first few miles, the sand turned into ankle twisting granite rocks.

The day finally finished at the nicely flowing Manter Creek. We got to camp early enough that we could rinse our feet and wipe the day's dust off of our legs. There are a lot of other campers at this site as we're only a short eight miles from Kennedy Meadows. This is a major milestone and the entrance to the Sierra.

Tomorrow we'll finish those last eight miles and prepare ourselves for the climb into the mountains.
