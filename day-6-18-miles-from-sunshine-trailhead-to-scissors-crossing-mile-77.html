Day 6 - 18 miles from Sunshine Trailhead to Scissor's Crossing (Mile 77)

As soon as our eyes opened this morning and we saw sunshine, we knew today was going to be a good day. After packing up and eating breakfast, we walked down to the highway to hitchhike back to the trail. After only a few minutes, a local piemaker picked us up and took us straight to be Sunshine Trailhead.

The parking lot looked completely different compared to the last time we were here. We could actually see! The first nine miles flew by as we enjoyed the sunshine and cool breeze. The view we had expected on Friday was finally visible and it didn't disappoint. We could see all the way to the desert floor. We were slowly dropping about 1500 feet and would eventually hit the valley below.

We stopped for lunch and refilled our water at mile 68. A number of other hikers were hanging around the precious water and we chatted about gear and the weather. Over the next nine miles, we would run into Silver, Two Watches, Dugan, and Sarah again.

After we finally reached the bottom of the mountain, we only had a few more miles until the road we planned to hitchhike on. The rocky chaparral of the mountainside became cactus and yucca as far as the eye could see. We met another hiker named Starfish and walked the last hundred yards to the road together. We invited her to come with us to the RV park, but she decided to try her luck the other direction on thth road.

Matt and I stuck our thumbs out and started walking. Our campsite was only about four miles away, but someone was bound to drive by and pick us up. After about ten cars and not even a hint of a bite, we just resigned ourselves to walking. That is until a state park ranger pulled up with Starfish in his car and offered us a ride. Finally!

We're now camped at a slightly strange RV park in the middle of the desert. We found Silver, Dugan, and Sarah already setting up camp when we arrived and Two Watches showed up later in the evening. The desert sky is gorgeous at night and the wind is pretty calm. I'm looking forward to sleep tonight.

Our goal for tomorrow is mile 94.5 with a potential walk back to the trail, so another twenty mile day is ahead of us. Then on Tuesday we head into the tiny town of Warner Springs to pickup our first resupply box.
